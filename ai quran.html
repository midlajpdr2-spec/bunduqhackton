Tafsir - Complete Quran Commentary

Explore all 114 surahs with detailed tafsir, translations, and interpretations. Click on any surah to see sample verses with commentary.

1
Al-Fatiha
The Opening 7 verses Makki
"In the name of Allah, the Entirely Merciful, the Especially Merciful."
This verse begins every surah except Surah At-Tawbah. It establishes that all actions should begin with Allah's name, seeking His blessings and guidance.
"All praise is due to Allah, Lord of all the worlds."
This verse establishes the fundamental principle that all praise and gratitude ultimately belongs to Allah alone, recognizing Him as the Creator and Sustainer of all existence.
"The Entirely Merciful, the Especially Merciful."
These two names of Allah emphasize His mercy - Ar-Rahman (Merciful to all creation) and Ar-Raheem (Especially Merciful to believers).
"Sovereign of the Day of Recompense."
This verse reminds us that Allah alone is the Master of the Day of Judgment, when all will be held accountable for their deeds.
"It is You we worship and You we ask for help."
This verse establishes the proper relationship between the servant and Allah - worship Him alone and seek help from Him alone.
"Guide us to the straight path."
This is a supplication for guidance to the straight path, which is the path of Islam and righteousness.
"The path of those upon whom You have bestowed favor, not of those who have evoked anger or of those who are astray."
This verse clarifies the straight path as the path of the prophets, the truthful, the martyrs, and the righteous, avoiding the path of those who earned Allah's anger or went astray.
2
Al-Baqarah
The Cow 286 verses Madani
"Alif, Lam, Meem."
These are the mysterious letters (Muqatta'at) that appear at the beginning of certain surahs. Their exact meaning is known only to Allah.
"This is the Book about which there is no doubt, a guidance for those conscious of Allah."
This verse establishes the Quran as the ultimate source of guidance for those who are mindful of Allah and seek righteousness.
3
Al-Imran
Family of Imran 200 verses Madani
"Alif, Lam, Meem."
These mysterious letters emphasize the divine origin of the Quran and its miraculous nature.
"Allah - there is no deity except Him, the Ever-Living, the Sustainer of existence."
This verse establishes Allah's unique attributes of life and self-sustenance, emphasizing His eternal nature and complete independence.
4
An-Nisa
The Women 176 verses Madani
"O mankind, fear your Lord, who created you from one soul."
This verse calls all humanity to consciousness of Allah, emphasizing our common origin and shared responsibility.
5
Al-Maidah
The Table Spread 120 verses Madani
"O you who have believed, fulfill [all] contracts."
This verse emphasizes the importance of keeping promises and fulfilling agreements, a fundamental principle of Islamic ethics.
6
Al-An'am

This verse begins with praise for Allah's creation, establishing the foundation of monotheism and divine sovereignty.
7

These mysterious letters serve as a reminder of the Quran's divine origin and miraculous nature.
8

This verse establishes the principle of disassociation from polytheists and those who violate treaties.

Ar-Rahman
The Most Merciful 78 verses Madani
"The Most Merciful."
This verse begins with one of Allah's most beautiful names, emphasizing His mercy and compassion.
"Taught the Quran."
This verse highlights Allah's mercy in teaching humanity the Quran as a source of guidance.
112
Al-Ikhlas
The Sincerity 4 verses Makki
"Say, 'He is Allah, [who is] One.'"
This verse establishes the fundamental principle of Islamic monotheism - Allah's absolute oneness.
"Allah, the Eternal Refuge."
This verse describes Allah as the Eternal Refuge, the One to whom all creation turns for their needs.
"He neither begets nor is born."
This verse negates any notion of Allah having offspring or being born, establishing His unique nature.
"Nor is there to Him any equivalent."
This verse emphasizes that nothing can be compared to Allah - He is absolutely unique and incomparable.
113
Al-Falaq
The Daybreak 5 verses Makki
"Say, 'I seek refuge in the Lord of daybreak.'"
This verse teaches seeking refuge in Allah from the darkness of night and the evils that may come with it.
"From the evil of that which He created."
This verse seeks protection from all evil that exists in Allah's creation.
"And from the evil of darkness when it settles."
This verse specifically seeks protection from the evils that come with darkness.
"And from the evil of the blowers in knots."
This verse seeks protection from those who practice magic and witchcraft.
"And from the evil of an envier when he envies."
This verse seeks protection from the evil eye and envy of others.
114
An-Nas
Mankind 6 verses Makki
"Say, 'I seek refuge in the Lord of mankind.'"
This verse teaches seeking refuge in Allah, the Lord of all humanity, from the whispers of Satan.
"Sovereign of mankind."
This verse emphasizes Allah's sovereignty over all human beings.
"God of mankind."
This verse establishes Allah as the only deity worthy of worship by all humanity.
"From the evil of the retreating whisperer."
This verse specifically seeks protection from Satan's whispers that retreat when Allah is remembered.
"Who whispers [evil] into the breasts of mankind."
This verse describes how Satan whispers evil suggestions into people's hearts.
"From among the jinn and mankind."
This verse clarifies that evil whispers can come from both jinn and human beings.
